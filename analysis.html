Problem Space

Music streaming changed how artists, labels, and listeners measure success. Instead of only looking at radio play or album sales, Spotify-style data lets us study streams, playlists, charts, and audio traits like energy, danceability, and valence. This project asks what popular songs tend to have in common and whether measurable audio features help explain streaming performance.

Dataset

The dataset contains popular Spotify songs from 2010 to 2019. It includes 41 songs and 26 original columns, including song, artist, genre, year, streams, playlist counts, chart appearances, BPM, mode, energy, danceability, valence, acousticness, speechiness, and popularity.

The cleaned CSV file is included as:

spotify_songs.csv

Questions to Address

  1. Which songs have the most streams?
  2. Which artists have the highest total streams?
  3. Which genres have the highest average popularity?
  4. How do streams change by year?
  5. Is danceability related to popularity?
  6. Is energy related to popularity?
  7. What song traits are most correlated with streams and popularity?
  8. Do major-mode and minor-mode songs differ in average popularity?

Final Insights

Spotify success is not explained by one feature alone. The highest-streamed songs usually combine broad pop appeal, playlist visibility, artist recognition, and strong replay value. Danceability and energy are useful traits to examine, but they do not guarantee high popularity. The strongest story from this dataset is that streaming success is a mix of audio features and platform exposure.
