Problem Space

This project studies what makes songs successful on Spotify. The analysis compares streaming totals with playlist placement, artist frequency, release timing, and audio features such as danceability, valence, energy, acousticness, liveness, and speechiness.

Dataset Connection

The Spotify dataset is useful because it contains both popularity measures and music feature measures. This lets us ask whether highly streamed songs are popular because of their sound, their visibility, or both.

Questions Addressed

  1. Which songs have the most Spotify streams?
  2. Which artists appear most often in the dataset?
  3. Do songs in more Spotify playlists tend to have more streams?
  4. What audio features are most common among popular songs?
  5. Is danceability related to streams?
  6. Is energy related to streams?
  7. Do newer or older songs dominate the most-streamed list?
  8. Which keys and modes appear most often?

Analysis Method

The Colab notebook imports the dataset into a Pandas DataFrame, cleans numeric columns, creates new columns such as streams in millions, uses summary statistics, filters and groups the data, and creates visualizations with Matplotlib.

Expected Insight Story

The project is designed to test whether audio features alone explain streaming success. The stronger expected story is that playlist visibility and platform reach matter a lot, while features like danceability and energy help describe songs but do not fully explain streams by themselves.
